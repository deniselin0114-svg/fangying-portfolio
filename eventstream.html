Introduction

What is Fabric?

Microsoft Fabric, one of Microsoft's fastest-growing analytics platforms, is a unified, cloud-based data platform that brings together everything needed to work with data — from data ingestion and transformation to data analytics and visualization — in a single experience.

It combines capabilities across data engineering, data science, real-time analytics, and business intelligence (including Power BI for reporting and insight sharing), all built on a shared data foundation.

Fabric also integrates AI and Copilot experiences to help automate tasks, generate insights, and accelerate data workflows.

By consolidating traditionally fragmented tools into one platform, Fabric enables teams to work on the same data without duplication or complex integrations. With AI assistance, Fabric empowers both technical and non-technical users to turn data into actionable insights faster.

Overview

A native, real-time streaming experience in Fabric

Eventstream is one of Microsoft Fabric's foundational Real-Time Intelligence experiences, designed to help organizations ingest, process, transform, and route streaming data from a variety of sources into analytics destinations in real time.

As one of the earliest workloads in Fabric, Eventstream played a critical role in establishing Fabric's vision of delivering a unified data platform where users can move seamlessly from data ingestion to analytics and business intelligence without leaving the ecosystem.

Built as a native Fabric experience, Eventstream enables users to create end-to-end streaming pipelines through a visual interface, reducing the complexity traditionally associated with real-time data systems.

Why we built it

Real-time data was powerful, but hard to reach

Modern organizations generate massive amounts of real-time data from applications, devices, databases, clickstreams, and cloud services. While this data holds significant business value, working with it often requires specialized expertise and multiple disconnected tools.

As Microsoft Fabric was being created as a unified data platform, there was an opportunity to rethink how users interacted with streaming data. Instead of forcing users to stitch together complex services and infrastructure, we wanted to create an experience where ingesting and moving real-time data could be as approachable as building a data report.

Eventstream was conceived as a foundational building block of Fabric — providing a simple, visual way to connect, transform, and route streaming data while integrating seamlessly with the rest of the platform.

Product vision

Make real-time data accessible to everyone

Our vision was to make real-time data accessible to more than just streaming specialists.

Through research with our primary persona data engineers and our secondary personas data analysts and data scientists, we identified a common challenge: building streaming data pipelines required specialized knowledge of distributed systems, event processing, and complex tooling.

To bridge this gap, Eventstream is designed to support multiple ways of building streaming solutions:

  1. A low-code visual experience that enables analysts and data scientists to create streaming topologies and data pipelines without requiring deep technical expertise.
  2. SQL and CI/CD support for data engineers and developers who prefer code-first workflows and need scalable, production-ready development practices.
  3. AI-powered natural language authoring with Copilot, allowing users to describe their intent in plain language and receive intelligent assistance when creating, configuring, and managing streaming pipelines.

By supporting visual, code-first, and AI-assisted experiences, Eventstream ultimately empowers users across different technical backgrounds to build and operate real-time data solutions more efficiently, and shorten the path from raw events to business insights and establish the real-time foundations.

As a result, users are empowered by Eventstream to:

  • Connect to 50+ in-house built live data sources
  • Transform and enrich streaming data in different ways depending on their technical background
  • Route events to multiple destinations — within the Fabric ecosystem or external sinks
  • Analyze data in real time and surface insights
  • Leverage AI-powered capabilities across the Fabric ecosystem
